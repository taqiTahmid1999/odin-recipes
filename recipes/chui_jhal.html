I love beef cooked with chui jhal

Description

Beef Chui Jhal is a vibrant and aromatic Bangladeshi dish that tantalizes the taste buds with its bold flavors and spicy kick. This dish features tender slices of beef cooked in a rich and tangy gravy infused with a medley of traditional spices.

The base of Beef Chui Jhal is formed by a fragrant blend of mustard oil and mustard seeds, which lend a distinct pungency to the dish. Sautéed onions, minced garlic, and ginger add depth and aroma, while chopped tomatoes contribute a subtle sweetness and help create a thick, luscious gravy.

The star of the show, the beef, is thinly sliced and simmered in the flavorful sauce until tender and infused with the robust flavors of the spices. The addition of green chilies provides a fiery heat that balances beautifully with the other ingredients, adding a fiery punch to every bite.

With its vibrant colors, aromatic spices, and bold flavors, Beef Chui Jhal is a dish that embodies the essence of Bangladeshi cuisine. Whether served with steamed rice or warm flatbread, this dish is sure to delight the senses and leave a lasting impression with its unforgettable taste.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams beef, thinly sliced
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 3-4 green chilies, sliced
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons mustard oil (or any cooking oil)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instruction

  1. Heat mustard oil in a pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add minced garlic and ginger, and sauté for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add sliced green chilies and chopped tomatoes. Cook until the tomatoes are softened and mushy.
  5. Add turmeric powder, chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Add the thinly sliced beef to the pan and mix with the spices. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the beef is partially cooked.
  7. Add a little water to the pan, cover, and let it simmer over low heat until the beef is tender and cooked through. Stir occasionally and add more water if needed to prevent sticking.
  8. Once the beef is cooked and the gravy has thickened, adjust the seasoning according to your taste.
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with steamed rice or flatbread.
